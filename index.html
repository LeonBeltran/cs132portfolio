The Stigma

Against LGBTQ

STDs have a negative stigma surrounding the LGBTQ+ community, in which social media equates being gay to contracting STDs. This negative stigma can go as far as using STDs as a derogatory term against the community and using the community as a scapegoat for the STDs. Upon further research, there is also a noticeable increase in this aggression against the community during and after an STD outbreak. So, this study aims to observe the trend and severity of this mindset on Twitter over a period of time.

The Plan

Problem

Have sexually transmitted diseases (STDs) such as HIV or AIDS, or diseases commonly attributed to STDs like monkeypox, caused an increased negative stigma on the LGBTQ+ community?

Null Hypothesis \(H_0\)

The diseases have caused an increase in the negative stigma against the LGBTQ+ community.

Alt Hypothesis \(H_A\)

The diseases have caused an increase in the negative stigma against the LGBTQ+ community.

Action Plan

Analyze the use and tone of language in tweets spreading mis/disinformation about the LGBTQ+ community regarding STDs or diseases attributed to STDs.

The Results

Statistical Test

Linear Regression

  • Pre-Monkeypox Period had a slope of \(0.0004\)
  • Post-Monkeypox Period had a slope of \(0.0028\)
  • This suggests that as time goes on, tweets in Post-Monkeypox era grow more aggressive than in Pre-Monkeypox era

T-test

  • with a confidence level of \(95\%\) (\(\alpha = 0.05\))
  • \(H_0\) was rejected (\(p = 3.20\times10^{-14} < \alpha \))
  • This suggests that there is a statistical significance between the time periods and tweet aggressiveness

Peak Finding

Peak Finding Parameters

  • Peak value considered if value is at least \(75\%\) of the max value in the graph
  • \(1\) peak was taken per year

Findings

  • A common peak was found between monkeypox and the Twitter mis/disinformation on July 2022 which is around the time of the monkeypox global outbreak
  • The term STD also shared this peak and AIDS had a peak 2 months later, September 2022
  • These findings suggest that the spread of mis/disinformation was possibly affected by the monkeypox outbreak.

To Conclude

Conclusion

The research was able to determine more aggression in posts after the boom of monkeypox during July 2022. We also found common peaks between the Google trend of the term monkeypox and LGBTQ+ mis/disinformation posts around the same time period. With these findings, we concluded that the monkeypox boom had a decent possibility of significantly affecting mis/disinformation on social media.

Recommendations
  • Use a larger sample size to get a more accurate result for the statistical tests.
  • Get a more equal distribution between tweets before and after the monkeypox period to get a less biased result
  • Look at other social media sights like Facebook to see if result carry across platforms.
  • Make use of a better measure of aggressiveness
